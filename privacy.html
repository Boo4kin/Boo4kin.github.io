ClipFlow Studio Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 1, 2026

1. Overview

ClipFlow Studio helps sports creators and editors prepare short-form clips from footage they own or are licensed to use. Users can review clip previews, edit captions, and publish selected videos to their own TikTok accounts after granting permission through TikTok Login Kit.

2. Data We Process

The service may process uploaded video files, generated clip files, titles, captions, hashtags, publishing settings, rights-confirmation choices, TikTok authorization status, upload status, and technical logs needed to operate the workflow.

3. How We Use Data

We use data to prepare clips, display previews, let users review and edit post metadata, submit selected videos through TikTok APIs, and show publishing status back to the authenticated user.

4. TikTok Authorization

When a user connects TikTok, TikTok may provide authorization tokens that allow ClipFlow Studio to submit videos only within the scopes the user approved. Users can revoke access from their TikTok account settings at any time.

5. Storage

Video files, queue data, generated metadata, and authorization tokens may be stored on secure application infrastructure or on a user's local workstation depending on the deployment. Tokens are protected and are used only for the authorized publishing workflow.

6. Sharing

We do not sell user data. Video content and metadata are sent to TikTok only when the user chooses to publish or upload a selected video through the ClipFlow Studio interface.

7. User Controls

Users can choose not to publish a clip, edit metadata before publishing, delete local project files, and revoke TikTok access in TikTok settings.

8. Contact

For privacy questions, use the support email listed in the TikTok Developer profile for ClipFlow Studio.
